🧪 Testing Strategy Board (Activity 6 — 3:30–4:15)

Hypotheses come from the concepts — not the other way around. For each selected concept: qual first to answer what only users can tell us, then A/B tests for the design decisions that become quantitative once we've narrowed.

🟢 Qual Test Plan — What we learn from users first

PER CONCEPT — KEY ASSUMPTION What must be true for this concept to work? (Example: "The co-pilot must feel like it knows the campaign, not just the category.")
WHAT WE SHOW USERS Prototype / story / concept sketch? What fidelity is right for what we're trying to learn?
WHAT WE'RE LISTENING FOR Not "do they like it?" — but: Does it feel campaign-specific? Does it give them more confidence? Does the moment it appears feel right? Does it feel like something they'd come back to?
WHO WE RECRUIT Local Hero / Political Change Agent / both? New starters or early-journey? How many sessions per concept?

🔵 A/B Test Roadmap — What we test quantitatively after qual

STRUCTURAL DECISIONS (good A/B candidates) Inline vs. dedicated surface · Phase-based vs. flat · 1 action vs. 3 actions shown · Guidance above or below the fold
TIMING DECISIONS What triggers guidance to surface · Prominence on return visit · When co-pilot speaks vs. stays quiet
CONTENT DECISIONS Momentum signal framing · How much context the co-pilot shows · Specificity of recommended actions · Whether it references similar campaigns
A/B TEST TEMPLATE If we [specific change], then [metric] will increase by [X%] because [reason]. Test: [Variant] vs. [Control]. Primary metric: Week 2 retention / guidance engagement / downstream action. Secondary: return visits / task completion.
